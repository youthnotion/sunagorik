About Sunagorik

Project Sunagorik, developed by Youth Notion, is a civic engagement platform designed to bridge the gap between the government and citizens in Bangladesh. It empowers users to report and resolve community issues efficiently through a user-friendly interface, voting systems, and data analysis. Key features include ASTRA, a real-time environmental monitoring system, a community directory, citizen journalism, and civic literacy programs. The platform aims to improve living conditions, enhance transparency, and promote accountability by fostering active citizen participation and immediate government intervention.

Why Sunagorik?

Bangladesh faces significant challenges in civic infrastructure, waste management, and environmental monitoring, compounded by limited communication between citizens and authorities. Sunagorik addresses these challenges by empowering citizens, enhancing government accountability, and promoting collaborative problem-solving. With features like ASTRA for real-time environmental monitoring, civic literacy programs, and citizen journalism, the project fosters transparency and data-driven decision-making.

Objectives

Sunagorik aims to empower citizens by bridging the gap between communities and authorities in Bangladesh. Through a civic engagement platform, it enables reporting and resolution of local issues, fosters accountability, and promotes collaboration with law enforcement and local governments. Integrating ASTRA for real-time environmental monitoring aligns with long-term goals of improving urban and rural living conditions, ensuring sustainable development, and enhancing transparency.

Who Benefits?

  • Citizens: Residents of urban and rural areas who use the platform to report issues and access real-time updates.
  • Local Government Authorities: Agencies responsible for addressing community issues.
  • Law Enforcement Agencies: Enhanced communication and data-driven insights.
  • Community Organizations: Tools for better collaboration.
  • Future Generations: Improved living conditions and sustainable urban development.
  • Environmental Stakeholders: Access to real-time environmental data.
  • Educational Institutions: Civic literacy programs for youth.
  • Policy Makers and Planners: Insights for policy and infrastructure development.
  • Media and Advocacy Groups: Reliable citizen-generated content.
